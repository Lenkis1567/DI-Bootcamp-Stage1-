Julio Balmaceda learned his first tango steps from his father, the legendary Miguel Balmaceda, and for many years accompanied him and helped him practice. After his death in 1991, Julio took over the classes at the most prestigious tango schools in Buenos Aires. In 1993 he opened the Parakultural with Omar Viola and in 1995 he was invited to participate in Alan Parker's film Evita. He was considered the youngest milonguero of his time.


He began his professional career in 1996 together with Corina De La Rosa, leading the new tango trends together with the traditional elegance of ballroom tango. They combined fluidity, subtlety, energy and dynamics. They were the soloist couple of the Color Tango Orchestra. In 1998, they were part of the hit Broadway production Forever Tango (Tony Award nominee for Best Choreography). In 1999, they were invited to dance at Carnegie Hall in New York, along with Gary Burton, Pablo Ziegler and Paquito D'Rivera, in homage to Astor Piazzolla.


In 2008 he choreographed his own show Corazón a Corazón in Tarbes, France. During 2011, he performed and choreographed the musical Tita, a Life in Tango Time directed by Nacha Guevara in Buenos Aires and was nominated for the ACE Awards as a musical choreographer. In 2013, he participated in the Tango Tranzas show in Sydney, Australia. Since 2003, he has been a jury member of the Buenos Aires Tango Festival and World Cup.


He started dancing with Virginia Vasconi in 2015, together they created a particular style that combined elegance with technique, and elasticity with strength..


Julio Balmaceda died on November 15, 2019. A benchmark of tango-dance, considered one of the most important teachers in the world due to his unique style and pedagogy.
